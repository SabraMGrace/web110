Social Media Optimization

Before the Internet existed, advertising budgets were insanely expensive. Regardless of how you feel about Facebook and selfies, social media helps businesses and customers find one another without needing to spend millions. Millions of ad dollars that would inevitably be passed on to consumers.

The first banner ad that anyone ever clicked their mouse upon debuted in 1994. 44% of the people who saw it clicked on it and found themselves staring at an AT&T webpage. Banner ads may have fallen out of fashion, but social media is the new evolution in connecting with customers online.

One third of Americans post or like something on social media more than 10 times a day. Social media also gives customers and businesses the chance to interact on a personalized level that has never existed before. In addition to targeted ads, you can reach the right demographic by using free tools like hashtags and attention-grabbing posts using specific color schemes, images and videos.

An infamous example of this is the Fyre Festival with its orange colored posts that sold the country on an event that didn’t exist. Obviously that is the dark end of the spectrum, but the point is that social media optimization is a powerful tool.

The aspect of social media that I like best is the connections that it fosters. As a business owner, you’ll be able to receive real time feedback from your customers. Social media makes it easy for customers to post positive comments and build a real relationship with your brand. Social media optimization is an important tool that enhances the value to your brand in nearly every way affordably and effectively.
